Get a job

  • - learn how to code / know the skills you need
  • - have a portfolio that proves you can do what you say you can do
  • - prepare for job search
    • - network
      • - online
        • - stackoverflow
        • - twitter
      • - meetups
      • - have soft skills (ie be able to talk to people)
    • - have a good resume
      • - education
      • - skills
        • - language used
        • - tools/frameworks
        • - list projects you've built
          • - name them
          • - explain what it does
      • - linkedin (essentially your cv)
      • - github (essentially your portfolio)
        • - neat code
        • - plus a readme
          • - what you learnt
          • - what it does
          • - any instructions
      • - job search
        • - communicate with network you are looking for a job
        • - career fairs
        • - apply online
  • - go through this process again and again and again
  • - interview process
    • - dont lie on your resume
    • - have your projects memorised / be able to talk about what you did and why
    • - be personable
  • - job offer
    • - negotiate salary (know your worth)
    • - stock, bonuses, decent benefits
  • - if you didnt get the job:
    • - rinse and repeat the steps
      • - keep working on skills
      • - keep networking
      • - keep job searching
    • - maintain and improve

Do 4 landing pages (using html/css/javascript)
Then start applying for jobs (after month 4)
Even before youre ready
It will give you interview skills
And even if youre not ready, those jobs will go anyway before you are ready, so theres no harm in applying. Its not like you'll stuff it up in the future. You're just gaining lots of skills and seeing what happens.
